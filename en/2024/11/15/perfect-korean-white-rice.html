Perfect Korean White Rice (흰쌀밥, White Rice, Heen Ssal Bap)

Chelsea Nov 15, 2024
  • rice
  • basics
  • pantry staples

Welcome back to Chelsea's K-Food Recipes! No Korean meal is complete without a perfect bowl of White Rice (흰쌀밥, Heen Ssal Bap). This simple but essential dish forms the base of many Korean meals, offering a fluffy, slightly sticky texture that pairs beautifully with banchan (반찬, side dishes), hearty stews, and grilled meats. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to cook Korean-style rice properly and how to choose the right type of rice for that authentic taste and texture.

🍚 Choosing the Right Rice

For authentic Korean-style rice, use short-grain or medium-grain rice. One of the most popular choices is Calrose rice, known for its soft and slightly sticky texture that makes it ideal for chopsticks and perfect for soaking up savory sauces. You can find Calrose rice in most Asian grocery stores, and using the correct rice truly makes a noticeable difference in flavor and consistency.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 2 cups Calrose rice (or other short-grain rice)
  • 2¼ cups water (adjust if needed based on rice type)

👩‍🍳 Instructions

1️⃣ Rinse the Rice

Place the rice in a bowl and fill with cold water. Gently swish the rice with your hand to remove excess starch, then drain the cloudy water. Repeat 3–4 times until the water runs mostly clear. This step helps ensure fluffy rice without overly sticky clumps.

2️⃣ Soak the Rice

After rinsing, soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain. Soaking allows the grains to hydrate properly and cook evenly.

3️⃣ Cook the Rice

🍚 Rice Cooker

Add the drained rice and water to the rice cooker. Start the cooker and wait for it to finish. Once done, let the rice rest for 5–10 minutes, then fluff gently with a rice paddle.

🔥 Stovetop

Add the rice and water to a pot with a tight-fitting lid. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to low heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the rice rest covered for another 10 minutes. Fluff before serving.

🍽 Serving Suggestions

Korean White Rice pairs wonderfully with classic dishes such as Bibimbap (비빔밥), Kimchi Stew (김치찌개, Kimchi Jjigae), Bulgogi (불고기), and everyday Korean banchan (반찬). Its clean flavor and soft, slightly sticky texture balance bold Korean flavors beautifully.

💡 Tips for Perfect Rice

  • Standard ratio: 1 cup rice : 1⅛–1¼ cups water
  • Always rinse thoroughly
  • Let the rice rest after cooking for the best texture
  • Fluff gently — don’t mash or stir aggressively
