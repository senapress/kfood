Stir-Fried Sausage and Vegetables (소시지 야채 볶음, Sausage Stir-Fry, Soseji Yachae Bokkeum)

Welcome back to Chelsea’s K-Food Recipes! Today we’re making a kid-friendly classic that’s perfect for lunch boxes — Stir-Fried Sausage and Vegetables (소시지 야채 볶음, Soseji Yachae Bokkeum).

This colorful stir-fry is sweet, savory, and packed with vegetables — a great way to add balanced nutrition to your child's meal. The key is using mild, low-sodium sausages so the flavors stay gentle and kid-approved. Let’s get started!

🌭 Ingredients

  • 4 mild sausages (chicken, turkey, or low-sodium)
  • 1/2 bell pepper (red or yellow), sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce (low-sodium recommended)
  • 1 tbsp ketchup
  • 1 tsp honey or sugar (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds (optional)

👩‍🍳 Instructions

1️⃣ Prep

Slice sausages into bite-sized pieces.
Cut all vegetables as directed — thin and even slices cook best for lunch boxes.

2️⃣ Cook the Sausage

Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
Add sausage slices and cook 3–4 minutes until lightly browned.
Remove and set aside.

3️⃣ Stir-Fry the Veggies

In the same pan, add onion, carrot, and zucchini.
Cook 2–3 minutes until slightly softened.
Add bell pepper and stir-fry 2 more minutes.

4️⃣ Combine & Season

Return sausages to the pan.
Add soy sauce, ketchup, and honey (if using).
Stir until evenly coated and cook 2–3 minutes until glossy.

5️⃣ Finish & Serve

Drizzle sesame oil, toss gently, and sprinkle sesame seeds if desired.

💡 Tips for Perfect Soseji Yachae Bokkeum

  • Choose mild or low-sodium sausages for kid-friendly flavor
  • Add broccoli, mushrooms, or corn for variety
  • Great warm or room-temperature — ideal for lunch boxes
  • Want a Korean lunch box vibe? Serve with rice, steamed egg, and fruit

This simple stir-fry is colorful, tasty, and veggie-packed — perfect for kids and adults alike. With gentle flavors and fun sausage bites, it’s a lunch box favorite you’ll make again and again.
