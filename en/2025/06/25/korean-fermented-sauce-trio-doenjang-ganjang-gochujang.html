The Trio of Korean Fermented Sauces: 된장 (Doenjang), 간장 (Ganjang), and 고추장 (Gochujang)

In Korean cuisine, the famous "장 트리오" (jang trio)—된장 (Doenjang), 간장 (Ganjang), and 고추장 (Gochujang)—forms the foundation of countless beloved dishes. Each one has its own deeply developed flavor, thanks to traditional fermentation techniques. Let’s take a closer look at what makes each sauce special and how they're used in everyday Korean cooking!

🫘 된장 (Doenjang) — Fermented Soybean Paste

What is Doenjang?

Doenjang is a thick, savory paste made by fermenting soybeans and salt over a long period. Its bold, earthy aroma and deep umami flavor give Korean soups and stews their signature taste.

Flavor Profile

  • Rich, salty, and deeply savory
  • Earthy and pungent
  • Powerful umami from natural fermentation

Common Uses

  • 된장찌개 (Doenjang Jjigae) — soybean paste stew
  • Ssamjang — mixed with gochujang for Korean BBQ
  • Soups, stews, dips, and marinades

Health Benefits

  • Packed with probiotics and enzymes
  • High in nutrients and gut-friendly goodness

🧂 간장 (Ganjang) — Soy Sauce

What is Ganjang?

Ganjang is Korean soy sauce—made as the liquid byproduct of fermented soybeans. It ranges from traditional Joseon ganjang to modern brewed versions called yangjo ganjang.

Korean soy sauce is typically saltier and less sweet than Japanese shoyu.

Flavor Profile

  • Salty and savory
  • Light texture
  • Slight sweetness depending on style

Common Uses

  • Seasoning almost every Korean dish
  • 간장계란밥 (Ganjang Gyeran Bap) — soy sauce egg rice
  • Marinades (like bulgogi)
  • Dipping sauces for dumplings and seafood

Health Benefits

  • Contains amino acids and minerals
  • High sodium — enjoy in moderation

🌶 고추장 (Gochujang) — Fermented Red Chili Paste

What is Gochujang?

Gochujang is a thick red chili paste made from red pepper flakes, fermented soybeans, glutinous rice, and salt. It’s spicy, slightly sweet, and full of complex flavor.

Flavor Profile

  • Spicy with sweet & savory notes
  • Thick, sticky texture
  • Slow-building heat

Common Uses

  • Bibimbap
  • Tteokbokki
  • Grilled meat marinades (고추장 불고기)
  • Soups and stews

Health Benefits

  • Contains capsaicin — may boost metabolism
  • Rich in vitamins and beneficial fermentation compounds

🥢 Summary

Sauce Flavor Texture Best Uses
된장 (Doenjang) Salty, earthy, savory Thick paste Stews, soups, ssamjang
간장 (Ganjang) Salty, umami Liquid Seasoning, marinades, dipping sauce
고추장 (Gochujang) Spicy, sweet, savory Thick paste Tteokbokki, bibimbap, marinades

These three fermented sauces are the heart and soul of Korean cooking. Mastering their flavors will open the door to countless Korean dishes—each with its own unique depth and warmth.
